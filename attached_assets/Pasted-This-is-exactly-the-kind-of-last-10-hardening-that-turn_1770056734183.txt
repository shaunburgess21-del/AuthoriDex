This is exactly the kind of “last 10%” hardening that turns a cool prototype into something you can trust in front of your brother (and later users). Replit implemented the right quick wins.

Here’s my read on what they just did, plus a few final tuning suggestions I’d keep in your back pocket.

What they just added is genuinely strong
✅ Spike baseline: mean → median (p50)

Great move. The mean is easily skewed by outliers (one crazy hour), which can either:

suppress spike detection (baseline gets inflated), or

create false spikes (baseline gets dragged weirdly)

Using p50 makes spikes much more “human-realistic.”

✅ Minimum delta thresholds (very important)

This is a big deal. Without minimum deltas, tiny accounts can trigger “breakout mode” from noise.

Their floors are sensible as a first pass:

Wiki: +5,000 pageviews

News: +10 articles

Search: +500 search volume

That will prevent “micro-noise breakouts.”

✅ Spike distribution logging

This gives you immediate “health telemetry”:
0:85, 1:10, 2:4, 3:1 is what you want in steady state.

If you ever see:

lots of 2s/3s constantly → threshold too low or baselines too low

almost never any 2/3 even during big news days → threshold too high

✅ Rank churn tracking

This is the best “does it feel organic?” metric.
A healthy engine usually has:

some churn in top 20

less churn in top 10

and churn spikes on major news days

The only thing I’d watch very closely now (for the next 24–48h)
1) Is the engine still “cap-dominated” after recalibration settles?

They expect “rate limited % dropping from 97% → <30%.” That’s a good target.

In steady state, I’d aim for:

<20–30% capped most hours

spikes in capped% when there’s a real global event

If it stays high even after [RECAL] expires, it means raw scores are still too jumpy.

If that happens, the best fixes (in order):

Smooth population stats slightly (light EMA on p50/p90 per source)

Loosen caps slightly in steady state (5% → 7%)

Increase per-source baselines to be less sensitive

Two tuning recommendations I’d add (small but high ROI)
A) Make minimum deltas relative to scale (not only fixed)

Fixed floors are good, but they don’t scale well across celebrities with wildly different typical volumes.

Better rule:

Spike if current > 1.5× baseline AND delta > max(fixed_floor, baseline × r)

Example:

for news: delta > max(10, baseline*0.25)
That means:

if baseline is 5 articles, require at least 10 (fixed floor)

if baseline is 200, require at least 50 additional articles (relative floor)

This prevents big names from spiking too easily on small relative changes, and small names from spiking on noise.

B) Add “churn guardrail” to detect something breaking

Rank churn is your canary.

Set an alert-style log:

If Top 10 churn > 4 or Top 20 churn > 8 in a normal hour → flag “possible data anomaly”

Dump the top 5 movers’ raw vs final and which sources drove them

This makes debugging instant when something weird happens.
