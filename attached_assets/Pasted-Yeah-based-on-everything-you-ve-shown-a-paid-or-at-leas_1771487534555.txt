Yeah — based on everything you’ve shown, a paid (or at least “production-grade”) news provider becomes important if news is going to stay a meaningful chunk of your score.

What you’re seeing now (GDELT returning “technically valid” but near-zero counts for specific people like Elon, plus “global near-zero” periods) is exactly the kind of brittleness that makes a free, unslatted endpoint painful in production. Your recent improvements (Serper fallback, governor, hysteresis, quality thresholds) are good engineering, but they’re still working around an unreliable upstream.

Do you need to switch away from GDELT?

Not strictly, but here’s the practical trade:

If you keep GDELT as primary, you’ll always be exposed to coverage cliffs and per-person weirdness (“Elon gets 0 while others don’t”) that won’t be fixed by a single global median trigger.

If you use a paid provider (or a provider with stronger infra + predictable quotas), you reduce the chance that your product UI looks “broken” (mass red days, top people sliding for non-organic reasons).

Given your use case (celebs / public figures, frequent refresh, leaderboard optics), I’d treat this as:
GDELT = great as a supplementary signal; a more reliable provider = primary.

The key point you raised about Serper fallback is valid

If your fallback logic is global (“median across everyone”), it can miss per-person failures. Even if you also implemented quality-based fallback, you still want per-person fallback as the real production fix:

If person’s news_count24h == 0 for N consecutive runs while wiki/search are normal, then fallback for that person only (Serper News or paid provider).

Store news_provider_used per person per snapshot (you already do) so debugging is instant.

That makes the system behave like: “news data is resilient per celebrity,” not “news data is resilient on average.”

Providers to consider (besides GNews + NewsAPI)

You’re already looking at two common options:

NewsAPI.org — widely used; check their plan limitations/terms carefully for your intended usage.

GNews.io — Google News–based; also check quota + commercial terms.

Other providers worth evaluating for your exact scoring-style use (counts + freshness + broad coverage):

Newscatcher (good for “mentions/counts” style use cases; often used for entity monitoring)

Event Registry (strong for event/entity-style news intelligence)

Mediastack (simple, cost-effective for basic “article counts”)

Webz.io (formerly Webhose; lots of sources, more “firehose” style)

Diffbot News / AYLIEN (more enterprise-y, but robust if you ever need entity extraction/sentiment too)

Also: don’t plan around Bing Search APIs for “news counts” — Microsoft retired Bing Search APIs (Aug 11, 2025), so it’s not a dependable path for new builds.

How I’d choose quickly (so you don’t overthink it)

For each provider, test the same 20 people (top 10 + 10 random) for:

Coverage: % with count > 0

Stability: variance of counts across 10 runs (hourly)

Latency + rate limits: can you comfortably ingest 100 people/hour?

Terms: caching, commercial use, displaying headlines/snippets, etc.

If you want one clean, production approach:

Use Paid provider (primary) for news counts

Keep GDELT (secondary) as a “bonus signal” when it’s healthy

Keep Serper News (tertiary) as emergency fallback

And in your engine:

Maintain news_provider_used per snapshot/person

Normalize news per provider (either separate percentile tables, or a provider scaling layer) so switching providers doesn’t silently shift the leaderboard.
