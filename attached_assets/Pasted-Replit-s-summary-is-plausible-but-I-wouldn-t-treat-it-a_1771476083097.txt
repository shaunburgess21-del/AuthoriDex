Replit’s summary is plausible, but I wouldn’t treat it as “all good” yet — there’s one big red flag:

The red flag

“19 consecutive failures due to global_zero detection” + “average news counts ~1”

That pattern usually means one of these is happening:

Provider is actually blacked out / failing (timeouts/429s/5xx), or

Your ingest path is “succeeding” but returning near-empty results (bad query shape, too-strict filtering, cache reuse returning empty, windowing bug, etc.), or

Your global_zero detector is too aggressive / mis-specified and is classifying “low coverage” as “outage,” keeping the news weight suppressed even when some news is available.

If news is truly “OUTAGE since Feb 18” while wiki+search are healthy, then continuous downtrend is expected because your velocity score is missing a big chunk of signal. So the behavior (scores slowly eroding) makes sense — but the diagnosis “global_zero” needs verification.

What I think this means for your engine

If you already have Serper News fallback + degradation governor, then a long-lived “OUTAGE” shouldn’t require users to eat a steady score decay unless:

the fallback isn’t triggering, or

fallback is triggering but produces near-zero counts, or

the governor is stuck in a “ramped-down” state and not recovering.

So: this is likely not just “normal erosion”—it’s probably a news subsystem stuck degraded.

Is fallback actually firing?

Health state recovery

Cache reuse side effects

With “proactive cache reuse,” confirm you are not repeatedly reusing empty/near-empty cached responses and calling them “success.”

Ideally: cache entries store result_count + fetched_at + source, and you avoid treating “0 results” as a healthy success unless you have strong confidence the query truly yields 0.

Do you need to switch to a paid news provider because of this?

Not automatically. If Serper News is integrated properly, it should prevent “everyone red” caused by news outages.

You should consider a paid provider only if:

Even with fallback + backoff + caching, you still see newsFreshCoveragePct < ~70% in >20% of runs, or

Your product needs consistent news coverage for credibility.

Right now, your priority is: make the system reliably detect and recover, and ensure fallback genuinely supplies non-zero signal.

My quick interpretation of Replit’s final line

“scores are gradually eroding as expected during an extended outage”

That’s only acceptable if:

you intentionally want “news missing = scores trend down”

and you’re comfortable with the UX of “whole board slowly bleeding red” during outages.

Most apps don’t want that. Instead, they:

downweight the missing source but renormalize, and/or

clamp per-run impact when a source goes missing (your governor), and/or

rely on fallback (Serper) so “outage” doesn’t feel like “platform dying.”
