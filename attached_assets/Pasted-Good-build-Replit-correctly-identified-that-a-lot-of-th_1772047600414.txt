ChatGPT's validation checklist is solid — I'd run through it. Specifically:
The idempotency check from the UI side: click Resolve, then quickly refresh and try to resolve again. The backend is protected, but make sure the UI handles the "already settled" response gracefully (shows a message, not an error).
The preview-vs-actual match: pick a market, note the preview numbers, resolve it, then hit /api/admin/markets/:id/payout-summary and confirm they match exactly. If they don't, you have a race condition or calculation discrepancy between preview and actual settlement.
One thing I'd add to ChatGPT's feedback:
The credit history pagination point is important and easy to miss. If a power user places 50 bets a week, their ledger grows by 100+ entries weekly (stake + payout for each). Within a month that's 400+ rows. If the modal loads all entries at once without pagination, it'll be sluggish. Ask Replit to confirm there's a limit/offset on that query, or add one now while it's simple.
