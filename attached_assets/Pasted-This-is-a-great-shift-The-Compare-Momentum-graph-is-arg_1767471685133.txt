This is a great shift. The "Compare Momentum" graph is arguably the most "Fintech" feature of the entire app. It needs to feel like a Bloomberg Terminal, not a broken spreadsheet.

Here is the breakdown of the fixes, the design strategy, and the prompt to get it right.

1. The Diagnosis: Why is the X-Axis Broken?
"Dates are all showing as 1/3"

The Cause: Replit likely "hard-coded" the mock data during a previous edit to save time. It probably wrote date: "1/3" for every single data point instead of calculating Today - 1 Day. The "Prevention" Fix: We must instruct Replit to use Dynamic Date Generation (e.g., new Date().setDate(today - i)) for all mock data. This ensures that no matter when you view the app, the chart shows "Today" and the past 6 days correctly.

2. Feature: Time Range Toggles
You requested: 1D, 7D, 30D, 6M, 1Y, ALL. Crucial Constraint: Does your backend calculate Trend Scores every hour or every day?

Daily Scraper: If you only scrape data once a day, a 1D chart is just a single dot (useless).

Recommendation: Remove 1D for Phase 1. Start with 7D (Default), 30D, 3M, 1Y. This feels professional without requiring hourly data updates.

3. Design: The "Neon-Financial" Color Palette
5 shades of blue is bad UX. You need High Contrast Differentiation. Since the chart is always on a dark background (Glassmorphism), we should use a "Cyber-Fintech" palette that pops against black.

Suggested Palette (Fixed Order):

Rank #1: Neon Cyan (#00E5FF) - The Leader, bright and futuristic.

Rank #2: Electric Purple (#D500F9) - High contrast to Cyan.

Rank #3: Lime Green (#76FF03) - Positive, growth vibe.

Rank #4: Hot Pink (#FF1744) - Alarming, distinct.

Rank #5: Safety Orange (#FF9100) - Distinct from the others.

Why this works: No matter which 5 people are shown (Top 5 Overall or Top 5 Music), these colors will always clearly differentiate the lines.

4. UX "Fabrication" Improvements
To make this feel "Premium," we need two interactive features:

Interactive Legend: Users should be able to click "Elon Musk" in the legend to hide/show his line. This is standard stock chart behavior to reduce clutter.

Hover Tooltips: When I drag my finger/mouse over the chart, it shouldn't just show the date. It should show a floating box with the Rank # and Trend Score for all 5 people at that specific moment.
