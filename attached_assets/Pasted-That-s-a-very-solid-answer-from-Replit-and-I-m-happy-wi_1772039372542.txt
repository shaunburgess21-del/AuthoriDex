2) Settle model clarity (pari-mutuel)

Pari-mutuel is a great fit for virtual credits because it’s self-balancing and you don’t create “house liability” problems.

The one thing you must get right is how you explain it in the UI, because users will otherwise assume fixed odds.

The UX/wording that prevents confusion

Wherever you show potentialPayout (or any “estimated return”), label it as:

“Estimated payout” (not “you will win”)

Add a tiny helper line: “Estimate changes as the pool changes.”

A simple line you can use everywhere:

Estimated payout — updates as more people bet.

What to show on the market card (simple version)

Pool size (total credits)

Your stake

“Estimated payout if correct” (dynamic)

Optional: “Share of winning pool” as a percent (nice mental model)

What to show on the bet confirmation (important)

Before confirming a bet:

“You’re staking: 50 credits”

“Current estimate if correct: ~120 credits”

“This may change as more bets are placed.”

That one sentence prevents 90% of future complaints.

One product call to make now: do you want “profit” or “net profit” on Top Predictors?

With pari-mutuel, a user might see “333 credits back” and think they “won 333,” but they staked 100. So net is +233.

For leaderboards, I strongly recommend:

Top Predictors ranked by Net P&L (payout − stake)

Display as +233 (not 333)

Then inside the bet history you can show both:

Stake: 100

Payout: 333

Net: +233

This keeps the leaderboard intuitive and comparable.
