ChatGPT's list is well-prioritized. Let me go through each with my honest take, then add what I think is missing.
1) Onboarding walkthrough — Agree, but keep it lighter than "3 screens."
A full coachmark tutorial feels heavy for a leaderboard product. Users expect to land and immediately understand what they're looking at. I'd do a single dismissible banner on first visit ("AuthoriDex tracks real-time celebrity attention using Search, News, and Wikipedia data. Rate people and predict trend movements to earn credits.") rather than a step-by-step tour. If someone needs 3 screens of explanation to use a leaderboard, the leaderboard itself needs work. That said, a "How It Works" page linked from the nav (for curious users) is worth having.
2) "What changed?" drivers card on person pages — Strongly agree, and you already have the data.
This is the highest-value item on the list for your specific product. You already store velocity components, search/news/wiki sub-scores, and diagnostics. Wiring a clean card that shows "Search ↑12%, News ↓3%, Wikipedia ↑8%" with the top headline makes every score movement feel transparent and earned. This is what separates "interesting product" from "trustworthy product." Prioritize this.
3) Watchlist + alerts — Agree on watchlist, defer alerts.
A simple "My Favorites" page showing starred people with their rank and 24h change is quick to build and gives users a reason to come back. You already have favorites infrastructure from the account page work. But alerts (notifications when someone enters Top 20, etc.) are a meaningful engineering effort — notification system, preference management, delivery mechanism. Defer that to post-launch. The watchlist alone is the retention hook.
4) Rate flow upgrade with distribution bar — Agree, high ROI.
Showing the community rating distribution (how many 1s, 2s, 3s, 4s, 5s) inside the rate modal makes voting feel social and grounded. It answers "am I the only one who thinks this?" which drives engagement. The "Based on N community ratings" line also adds credibility as the number grows. This is a small frontend change with outsized impact on how voting feels.
5) Anti-confusion labels on leaderboard rows — Agree, very quick win.
Adding "24h" next to the movement percentage is a one-line change that prevents confusion. Right now someone seeing "+12.1%" has to infer what time period that refers to. Making it explicit costs nothing.
6) Market lifecycle polish — Strongly agree.
This is the prediction UX equivalent of what you did with the trend score tooltips. After a market resolves, immediately showing "You won +150 credits" or "You lost 50 credits" on the market card is critical. Users shouldn't have to navigate to their account to find out what happened. The "My position" strip on markets you've bet on is also important — it answers "did I already bet on this?" at a glance.
7) Abuse tripwires — Agree on rate limits, defer pattern detection.
Max bets per minute and max stake per market are simple server-side checks that prevent obvious exploits. With virtual credits the stakes are low, but you still don't want someone draining 10,000 credits into a market in one second. The pattern detection (betting both sides, new account large stakes) is more complex and can wait until you have real usage data showing actual abuse patterns.
8) Skeletons + optimistic updates — Agree, but don't boil the ocean.
Consistent skeleton loading states make the product feel polished. The leaderboard and prediction pages are the two highest-traffic views — make sure those have skeletons. Optimistic updates on vote submission and bet placement are also worth doing. But don't go through every component retroactively adding skeletons — just hit the main flows.
What ChatGPT missed that I'd add:
Social proof / activity feed on the homepage. Right now your homepage has "Daily Movers," "Weekly Gainers," and "Weekly Droppers" — which are good. But adding a lightweight real-time activity feed ("User X just predicted Elon Musk will trend Up" or "User Y rated LeBron James 5/5") would make the site feel alive, especially early when traffic is low. Even showing the last 10-15 actions gives the impression of an active community. This is a common pattern on prediction and trading platforms for exactly this reason.
Share functionality. When someone is #1 on the trend leaderboard or wins a big prediction, let users share that to social media. A "Share" button on person profiles ("Elon Musk is trending #2 on AuthoriDex") and on prediction results ("I won +300 credits predicting Drake would trend up!") is free marketing. Even a simple copy-to-clipboard share link is valuable.
