Yeah — your instinct is right: Score Drivers was the one thing that could quietly undermine trust if it looked “math-y but wrong.” Replit’s fix is the right direction and (if implemented as described) should make it feel sane.

Here’s my take on what they did, what’s good, and the one improvement I’d still recommend.

Is Replit’s fix a good idea?
✅ Yes, the two changes they made are exactly what you want

Tighten the “stable” threshold
If you don’t have meaningful movement, showing bars is worse than showing nothing. Collapsing to “Stable — no major signal shift” is the correct UX.

Add a lopsided guard (the >90% single source rule)
This specifically kills the “0/0/100” visual that screams “bug,” even if it’s technically coming from tiny non-zero deltas. Great safety net.

Also: renaming to “Score Drivers (24h change)” + tooltip is a must. That framing is what prevents confusion.

The small red flag (still worth fixing)

Their logic uses:

totalAbsDelta threshold (now 0.15 instead of 0.05)

AND a “lopsided + small change24h” collapse rule (>90% and <3% change)

That’s fine, but it’s still a bit heuristic-heavy. The more robust approach is to base “drivers” on a quantity that directly matches what users see:

Better rule of thumb (more truthful)

Only show Score Drivers when the person’s actual 24h score move is meaningful.

For example:

show drivers only if abs(change_24h) >= 2% (or 1.5%)

otherwise show “Stable”

This keeps the drivers tied to something users already understand (“24h change”), and avoids edge cases where deltas are non-zero but the score barely moved.

Even better (if you want one extra step)

Use both:

abs(change_24h) >= 2% OR

totalAbsWeightedDelta >= threshold

That gives you a precise model-based gate and a user-facing gate.

What caused “0/0/100” in the first place?

Your deltas can be tiny-but-nonzero due to EMA smoothing and caching behaviors. When two sources are near-zero and one has even a tiny non-zero, it becomes 100% of “what changed”—mathematically correct, visually misleading.

So hiding that under “Stable” is absolutely correct.

The other improvements they listed

“flat” vs “no change” ✅ Much better language.

Jump to Signals ✅ Great especially on mobile.

Related Searches / People Ask ✅ This will become your most shareable “wow” moment once data is flowing.

Single profile inline ✅ Small polish, but makes the page feel cleaner.

My one recommendation to send back to Replit

Ask them to switch the drivers gating to be primarily based on abs(change_24h), not only totalAbsDelta.

Suggested final gate:

If abs(change_24h) < 1.5–2% → Stable

Else show drivers.

That makes Score Drivers feel “obviously correct” to users.
