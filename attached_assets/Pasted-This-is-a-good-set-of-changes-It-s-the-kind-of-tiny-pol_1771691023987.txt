This is a good set of changes. It’s the kind of “tiny polish that prevents confusion” you want in Phase 1.

1) Acronym whitelist — yes, do it

Without it, your fallback chips will feel randomly empty on exactly the pages users care about (sports, crypto, geopolitics). Letting through a curated set of short tokens keeps the section feeling “alive” even when Serper doesn’t return related searches.

Only caveat: keep the whitelist tight and explicit (which Replit did). Don’t let it become a generic “allow any 2–3 letter token,” or you’ll reintroduce junk chips.

2) “Compared to ~24h ago” micro-label — also yes

This is a UX win because it resolves the biggest mental mismatch users have:

they see a score change

then see drivers

and assume it’s explaining “who is popular” rather than “what caused the movement”

That one line prevents loads of support questions and makes the drivers card feel more credible.

A quick “way forward” checklist (to keep this tight and trustworthy)
A) Make the Search Interest card feel fuller (without extra APIs)

Even with topic chips, I’d add one more line that uses data you already have:

“Search activity score: 69/100” (already there)

Add “Trend: steady / rising / falling” based on your search delta over ~24h (you now compute this)

If Serper related searches are empty, show:

Topics in the news chips (already)

plus a small hint: “Top searches appear when available”

That alone makes it feel less “blank card”.

B) Drivers should always reflect what users see (you’re on the right path)

Storing per-source velocity components and labeling Exact vs Estimate is the correct long-term fix. You’ll stop arguing with your own UI.

One thing to watch: don’t show “Exact” unless both snapshots (now and ~24h ago) are on the same driversModel version. Sounds like you already added that guard.

C) Freshness for the bottom half of the leaderboard

Your current approach is fine as long as you:

keep source-level freshness visible (“Sources active: 2/3” etc.)

show quiet vs stale states (quiet = “nothing moved”, stale = “data delayed”)

For “non-trending” people, users don’t need constant novelty — they need honest stability that still looks intentional.

My verdict on Replit’s build direction

✅ Correct product call: Momentum Signals > Platform Insights
✅ Correct engineering call: no scrapers, no expensive follower APIs
✅ Correct trust call: drivers explain movement, not fame
✅ These two micro-changes (acronyms + ~24h label) are exactly the kind of UX glue that turns “cool prototype” into “feels real”

If you want one more “high impact, low cost” improvement next: add a tiny tooltip on Search Interest:
“Search Interest is a composite activity score, not follower count.”

That further reduces confusion.
