I agree with your instinct to avoid “rank matters more than the number” (it can feel like you’re dodging), but I wouldn’t go too vague either — if it’s too vague, it can trigger the opposite reaction: “so it’s made up?”

The sweet spot is:

Clear inputs (3 signals)

Clear provenance (“third-party data sources”)

Clear cadence (updates regularly / hourly)

Clear meaning (“higher = more attention right now”)

No formula details (no normalization, weights, proprietary steps)

My recommended copy (tight, credible, non-gameable)

How Trend Score Works

Trend Score measures real-world attention using three independent signals:

Search interest — how often people search for them

News coverage — how often they appear in recent articles

Wikipedia activity — how often people read about them

We combine these third-party signals into a single score that updates as new data comes in. A higher score means more attention right now.

Sources: Search • News • Wikipedia

That last line (“higher score means more attention right now”) is important. It answers the significance question without explaining how you got 368,443.

Why I like this more than your draft

Your draft:

“combined and aggregated… affect rank and movement…”

It describes the mechanism (leaderboard movement) but not the meaning of the score. People still go: “okay… but what does 368,443 mean?”

Adding one plain-English meaning sentence fixes that.
