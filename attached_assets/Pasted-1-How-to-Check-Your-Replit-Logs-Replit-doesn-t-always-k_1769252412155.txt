1. How to Check Your Replit Logs
Replit doesn't always keep old logs forever (unless you wrote them to a file), but you can check the recent history to see what happened.

If you are running the bot in the Editor (Run Button):

Open your FameDex project in Replit.

Look at the Console tab (usually on the right side).

Scroll up as far as you can.

Look for the Error:

The "429" Error: Search (Ctrl+F or Cmd+F) for 429 or Too Many Requests. This confirms you hit the rate limit.

The "Loop" Check: Look for repeated blocks of text that look identical and appear very frequently (e.g., every second instead of every 8 hours).

Example of a loop: If you see [INFO] Fetching trending people... appearing hundreds of times in a row without a long pause between them, your setInterval or sleep function failed, and it ran continuously.

If you are using Replit Deployments (Always On):

Click the Deployments button (rocket ship icon) in the sidebar.

Click on your active deployment.

Click the Logs tab.

This will show you the historical output of your server. This is the best place to find the evidence if the crash happened while you were asleep.

2. How to Get the X API Usage Graph
This is the "smoking gun" screenshot you need to attach to prove you aren't trying to trick them.

Go to the X Developer Portal.

On the left sidebar, look for Projects & Apps.

Click on your specific App name (e.g., "FameDex App").

Look for a section called Usage or Dashboard.

The Trap: Ensure you are looking at the "Tweets Pulled" (or "Posts retrieved") metric, not just "Requests."

Note: The Basic tier often limits you to 10,000 posts retrieved per month. If your app pulls 100 profiles x 10 tweets each = 1,000 posts per run. If you run that 3 times a day, you hit the 10,000 limit in 3 days.

Take a Screenshot: Capture the graph showing the sudden spike and the flatline (the ceiling).

3. Summary of what to look for
If the graph is a vertical line straight up: You had a loop bug (code error).

If the graph is a steady staircase that ends too soon: You misunderstood the "Tweets vs. Requests" math (logic error).
