Overall: the direction is good (canonical gating + removing Cooling from Hot Movers), but I’d be a bit careful about merging the badges and deleting Cooling globally the way Replit implemented it.

Here’s my take, point by point.

✅ #1 Canonical “Why Trending” fix — excellent

Replit did the right fix: stop depending on /movers/daily to decide whether the profile should show the Why Trending card.

Two key wins:

Same dataset + same thresholds as the homepage/leaderboard → no more “badge on board but no card on profile”.

Using the same TanStack Query cache key for the “thresholds-full” leaderboard dataset is smart (fast + consistent).

One extra thing I’d still ask them to confirm:

If the AI summary is “generated on demand”, make sure it doesn’t silently fail when the person is eligible but the summary hasn’t been generated yet. Ideally you want:

Loading state

Retry

Friendly fallback (“Generating summary… check back in a few seconds”)

But conceptually: this fix is correct and scalable.

✅ #2 Cooling removed from Hot Movers — agree

This is consistent with your product language.

Hot Movers should be about:

“something is happening”

“gaining attention”

“opportunity to explore why”

Cooling is the opposite.

So removing Cooling from Hot Movers eligibility and from the “Why Trending” trigger makes sense.

However…

⚠️ I wouldn’t necessarily delete Cooling everywhere

Replit’s implementation sounds like they removed Cooling from the badge system entirely (“no badge or trigger the card”). That might be too blunt.

Cooling can still be useful on the main leaderboard as a subtle indicator (not “hot”), because:

it provides contrast and “story”

it makes the system feel alive (things go up and down)

it’s still informative for power users

The clean compromise:

Cooling stays as a leaderboard badge (optional/subtle)

Cooling does NOT appear in Hot Movers

Cooling does NOT trigger Why Trending

That preserves signal without breaking your “Hot Movers” promise.

⚠️ #3 Merging Spiking + Rising into Surging — good UX, but check for “loss of meaning”

I agree with the motivation: users won’t naturally internalize three similar labels.

But there’s a risk:

“Surging” becomes a bucket that includes two very different realities:

Score spike (true attention surge)

Rank jump (relative movement)

Replit says they’ll show “driver shown in tooltip” — that helps. But I’d insist on one extra tweak:

Make “Surging” visibly two subtypes (without adding new badges)

You can keep one label, but add a tiny cue:

Surging · Score (or a small ⚡ icon)

Surging · Rank (or a small ↗ icon)

Even if it’s only inside the tooltip (or as a mini pill), it prevents “Surging” from feeling vague.

Also: merging should not change the underlying logic in a way that suddenly makes too many people qualify. Watch for badge inflation. If half the board is “Surging”, the badge loses value.

Big potential issue: they changed the legend and the system, not just Hot Movers

From your paste, it sounds like Replit:

changed getExceptionalIndicator globally,

updated the homepage legend,

removed Cooling globally.

That’s a product decision, not just a “Hot Movers cleanup”.

If your goal was specifically: Hot Movers should correlate perfectly with leaderboard badges and Why Trending should always show when it should…
…then the minimal change would have been:

Fix #1 canonical gating ✅

Remove Cooling only from Hot Movers ✅

Leave the rest alone until you decide.

So: if you’re happy with a simpler 2-badge system, fine — but recognize that you’ve just changed your product vocabulary everywhere, not just in one widget.

What I’d do next (quick sanity checklist)

Confirm correlation:

If someone appears in Hot Movers, they must have the same badge on the leaderboard (still true).

Badge distribution check:

What % of the top 100 now show “Surging”?

If it’s >15–20% consistently, thresholds might be too loose.

Cooling decision:

Decide explicitly: do you want Cooling on leaderboard or not?

If yes: keep it there, just exclude it from Hot Movers + Why Trending triggers.

Why Trending loading fallback:

Ensure summary generation failures are visible and recoverable.
