What’s probably happening with “Replit uses OpenAI”

Replit’s AI features often run through their own integrated LLM routing (could be OpenAI, Anthropic, etc., depending on product/settings). Even if it feels “free,” there’s still a runtime cost + latency somewhere — either billed to you, bundled into Replit, or rate-limited behind the scenes.

You don’t need perfect details to design it correctly — you just need to make your implementation:

cached

idempotent

not regenerated too often

traceable

Your current logic (top 10 only) is strong — two “gotchas” to watch
1) Thrash near the boundary

If rank #10 and #11 keep swapping hourly, you’ll regenerate text too often unless you add hysteresis.

Fix: only (re)generate when one of these is true:

newly enters top 10 and hasn’t had a summary in the last X hours

summary is older than TTL (e.g., 6–12 hours)

a “drivers changed” signal (e.g., top driver source changed, or big delta)

2) Inconsistent summaries from different runs

LLMs can paraphrase differently each time. Users may notice “why trending” changes even when the data didn’t.

Fix: lock it to a stable input bundle and cache it.
