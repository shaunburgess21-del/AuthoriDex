This is a really solid follow-up build — it addresses the exact “sharp edges” you’d otherwise discover only once real users start betting.

Here’s my quick audit of what Replit shipped, plus the only things I’d double-check.

✅ What’s great here
T002 “Awaiting Results” strip

Perfect UX. Users hate ambiguity more than losing. This makes the closed→resolved gap feel intentional, not broken.

T003 “My Positions (N)” filter

This is a big usability upgrade once there are many markets. Bonus points for only showing it when:

user is logged in and

they actually have bets

That avoids clutter for new users.

T001 Multiple bets aggregation

This is important. Without it, you get confusing outcomes like:

“Won” on one bet, “Lost” on another, and the UI lies depending on which row you happened to fetch.

Summing stakes/payouts + “Multiple positions” is the right first version.

T004 User-facing payout breakdown endpoint

Also very good — and the right scoping:

authenticated only

returns just what that user needs (pool totals + their stake + payout)

This is exactly how you build trust without exposing other users’ positions.

T005 friendlier rate limit copy

Nice polish. Keep it friendly.

🔍 Two things I would verify (fast checks)
1) Security of /api/markets/:id/my-payout

Make sure it is requireAuth and uses the authenticated user ID server-side — not a passed parameter.

Also make sure it doesn’t leak:

other users’ stakes

identities

per-entry totals if you consider that sensitive (pool totals are fine; “winner pool share” is fine; just don’t reveal competitor lists)

2) “My Positions” filter includes all bet states you want

Decide the product behavior:

Should it include resolved markets you participated in? (I think yes, for a short period.)

Should it include closed-pending? (yes)

Should it include voided/refunded? (maybe hide by default, but accessible in “History”)

If the filter is strictly “active bets only,” you may confuse users who placed bets and then the market resolved—they toggle “My Positions” and their bet disappears. A good default is:

show markets where user has any bet with status in {active, won, lost, refunded, pending_resolution}

🎯 One small UX improvement to make this feel “premium”

On the “View details” payout breakdown, show the numbers in the same order every time:

Your stake: 120 credits

Outcome: Won / Lost / Refunded

Your payout: 240 credits

Net P&L: +120 credits

Total pool: 1,000 credits

Remainder: 0 (rounding)

Users think in “what did I win/lose?” first, not in pool mechanics.

✅ Overall verdict

This is exactly the kind of iteration that makes the prediction section feel trustworthy and easy to use:

outcomes are visible on the card,

ambiguity is handled (“Awaiting Results”),

users can focus on their own positions,

and the system explains payouts without oversharing.
