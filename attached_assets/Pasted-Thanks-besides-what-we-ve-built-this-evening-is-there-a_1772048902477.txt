Yep — if you want high-ROI enhancements that improve “launch readiness” without dragging you into a 2-week rabbit hole, here are the best ones to ask Replit for (in priority order).

1) Onboarding that actually teaches the product (2–3 screens)

Most users won’t “discover” Vote + Predict + Trend Score logic organically.

First visit (or first login): a 3-step coachmark walkthrough:

“This is the Trend Score leaderboard” (what it means)

“Tap Rate to vote 1–5” (what that affects)

“Predict with credits” (what the user leaderboard is)

Add “Don’t show again” + “Replay tour” in settings.

Why it matters: reduces confusion/support, increases first-session activation.

2) “What changed?” mini-panel on each person (trust + engagement)

On a person page, add a small, clean “Drivers (last 24h)” card:

Search Interest: ↑/↓ and value

News volume: ↑/↓ (plus top 1 headline)

Wikipedia: ↑/↓

Trend Score: Δ%

You already store a lot of diagnostics — this is mostly UI wiring.

Why it matters: makes jumps feel earned and reduces “is this organic?” doubts.

3) Watchlist + alerts (simple version)

Let users star people and get:

A Watchlist page (just their favorites + rank + 24h change)

Optional alerts:

“Notify me if they enter Top 20”

“Notify me if 24h change exceeds X%”

“Notify me if rank changes by ≥ Y”

Start with in-app notifications only; add email/Telegram later.

Why it matters: retention loop. People come back for their list.

4) Rate flow upgrade: “Rate distribution” + “your last rating”

Inside the Rate modal:

Show a tiny distribution bar (how community rated 1–5)

Show “You rated: 4/5 (tap to change)”

Add a “rating confidence” helper line: “Based on N community ratings”

Why it matters: clarifies what approval % means, makes voting feel grounded.

5) Anti-confusion labels on Leaderboard rows

Keep rows clean, but add microcopy cues:

“24h” label aligned with the 24h movement number (so it’s not ambiguous)

If you ever show approval again in rows, display it as a subtle badge with label “Approval” — never as a naked %.

Why it matters: prevents the “two percentages = huh?” issue.

6) “Market lifecycle” polish (predict UX)

On market cards:

Show status clearly: Open / Closing Soon / Closed Pending / Resolved

“Closes in …” countdown

After resolution: show “Outcome + your result” immediately:

Won + payout

Lost + stake

Add a “My position” strip on every market you’ve bet on.

Why it matters: prediction products live/die on clarity.

7) Lightweight abuse tripwires (credits economy)

You already have ledger + reconciliation. Add:

Simple rate limits: max X bets per minute, max stake per market, max stake per hour (configurable)

Flag patterns:

same user betting both sides (if allowed, show warning)

new account placing large stake immediately

Why it matters: avoids obvious exploits before they become drama.

8) Performance/UX: skeletons + optimistic updates everywhere

You’ve done some. Make it consistent:

Leaderboard loads: skeleton rows

Rate submission: optimistic “Your rating saved” + instant UI update

Predict placement: optimistic stake deduction + “Pending” state until confirmed

Why it matters: the site feels “fast” even when it isn’t.
