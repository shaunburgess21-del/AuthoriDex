What you’re describing is exactly what a “mostly-correct” trend engine looks like when one external source is flaky AND your pipeline reacts too aggressively to partial failures. The good news is: the fixes Replit just shipped are the right ones to stop the daily whiplash.

Here’s the clean breakdown.

Why it kept looking “wrong”
1) GDELT partial failures were the real chaos trigger

The key line from your logs is:

News fallback: 85/100

That means in a single run:

15 celebs got fresh news counts

85 celebs got stale carried-forward news counts

So you had an “apples vs oranges” ingestion hour (mixed freshness across the population). That alone can reshuffle ranks.

2) Weight renormalization made a partial outage become a full leaderboard recalculation

When Replit was redistributing weights during News OUTAGE:

News weight → 0

Search/Wiki weights jump up

That means everyone’s score formula changed, even if their underlying data didn’t. That’s why you saw crazy moves like #96 → #1.

Removing redistribution was the correct fix.

3) Recalibration mode + higher caps amplified it

If recalibration was still active, you effectively allowed much bigger hourly movement during a period when data quality was already inconsistent.

Disabling recalibration was also the correct call for “normal operation.”

Do the current fixes actually solve “why can’t it just work?”

Yes — the two biggest stability killers are now removed:

✅ Recalibration disabled (caps back to normal behavior)
✅ No weight redistribution (formula stays consistent even during outages)
✅ GDELT timeouts/retries improved (fewer partial runs)

This combination is exactly how you stop a ranking system from “swinging the whole league table” because one feed had a bad hour.

Will GDELT keep failing in the future?

Yes, occasionally. Not because you’re “flagged,” but because:

GDELT is free, public, research-grade

No SLA

Can 429 (rate-limit) or timeout under load

Sometimes has TLS/network instability depending on the endpoint and your host environment

The important part is this:

With your new architecture, a GDELT failure should no longer cause chaos.

When GDELT fails now, you:

keep weights fixed

use cached/fill-forward values

avoid population-wide recalculation

So it becomes “news is stale for a bit” rather than “leaderboard exploded.”

One more improvement I’d strongly recommend (so you don’t keep firefighting)

Right now, your biggest remaining risk is partial freshness (e.g., 15/100 fresh, 85/100 fallback). Even without weight redistribution, partial freshness can still produce weird relative moves.

Add a “coverage gate” per source per run:

If GDELT returns fresh values for fewer than, say, 70% of celebs:

treat News as DEGRADED for the entire run

and use previous hour’s news values for everyone, consistently

This gives you population consistency, which is what makes rankings feel “fair.”

You already have source health — this is just making it stricter:

either most people get fresh news,

or nobody does that hour.

That one rule eliminates a huge chunk of perceived weirdness.

What you should expect “tomorrow” if everything is now healthy

Top 10 should not roulette-spin hour to hour

Movers widgets should show plausible changes

If GDELT breaks again, you should see:

banner saying News is degraded/outage

but ranks remain mostly stable (no #96 → #1 events)

If you still see dramatic swings after these fixes, it’s usually one of:

partial freshness without a coverage gate

stale hourly snapshots “frozen” by .onConflictDoNothing() when a bad run happens early in the hour

a bug in spike detection thresholds after a scale change (you already went through this with Serper)

My recommendation on “production readiness”

For launch, you can absolutely keep GDELT as long as you have:

fixed weights (done)

cached fallback + staleness decay (done)

source health + banner (done)

coverage gate (recommended)

(optional) a paid backup news API later when budget allows

If you want a backup later, pick one with predictable rate limits and reliability (even a low-tier plan) and only use it when GDELT is OUTAGE for multiple hours.
