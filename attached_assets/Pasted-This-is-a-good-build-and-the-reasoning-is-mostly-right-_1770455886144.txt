What they did that’s 100% correct
1) Chart fix: move to ISO timestamps + smarter tick formatting

✅ This is the right approach. If you plot time strings (“09:00 AM”) you’ll always get confusing repeats. Using an ISO timestamp as the x value, then formatting it on the client per range, is the correct architecture.

Also good: adding a “date+time” label at midnight crossings so you can visually see the boundary.

The Wikipedia change: good intent, but watch the tradeoff

Replit’s “use 7-day avg for wiki velocity normalization” will remove the daily cliff-edge, but it also makes wiki less responsive as a velocity signal (because 7-day avg barely moves hour to hour).

Whether that’s correct depends on what you want wiki to represent:

If wiki is “mass/baseline fame”

Then ✅ yes — wiki should primarily live in mass, and should not create hour-to-hour momentum swings.

If you want wiki to also contribute to “velocity”

Then I’d prefer this instead of 7-day avg:

Fetch hourly Wikipedia pageviews and compute a rolling 24h sum (or rolling 6h/12h) yourself.

That gives you a true velocity-like wiki signal without daily resets.

So: their change is a solid “ship now” fix to eliminate artifacts, but later you’ll probably want hourly wiki if you want wiki to meaningfully affect momentum.
