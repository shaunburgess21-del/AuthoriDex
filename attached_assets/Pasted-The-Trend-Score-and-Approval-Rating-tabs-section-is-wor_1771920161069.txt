Totally agree with your brother on both points:

It’s eating vertical real estate while you scroll (and on mobile, height is everything).

The (i) inside the pill feels like a random extra element inside a control that should be “tap anywhere to switch ranking”.

Here are a few strong design options (from simplest → cleanest), plus wording you can send to Replit.

What I’d change (high confidence)
1) Make each tab a single-line “chip”

Right now the height is mainly coming from wrapped labels (“Trend / Score”, “Approval / Rating”).

Fix:

Force no wrap: whitespace-nowrap

Reduce font size slightly on mobile: text-sm (or even text-[13px])

Use shorter labels:

Trend (or “Trend Score” but one-line)

Approval (instead of “Approval Rating”)

If you still want full meaning, you can keep the full label but shrink the font.

Result: tabs drop to a single row with consistent height.

2) Remove the left-side icons inside tabs (crown / thumbs-up)

They’re not adding clarity anymore, and they cost space + create visual noise.

Recommendation: remove them entirely from the tabs.

If you really want an icon, keep it only on the active tab (subtle), but I’d try no icons first.

3) Move the info (i) OUTSIDE the toggle pills

This is the biggest “feels weird” fix.

Instead of each pill containing its own (i), do:

Tabs = only switching

One small info icon to the right of the whole control (or in the “Sorted by” line)

Example layout:

[ Trend ] [ Approval ] (i)

Then the info tooltip content can change based on active tab:

If Trend is active: explain Trend Score

If Approval is active: explain Approval Rating

That gives you:

cleaner toggle

fewer targets

less clutter

less height
