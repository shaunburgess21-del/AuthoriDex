Why item 1 (stale snapshot guard) is worth doing now

When you’ve had downtime (Replit sleeping), comparing “current vs previous” can turn into “current vs 13-hours-ago,” which will create big deltas that look like a sudden spike even though it’s just a gap. Zeroing searchDelta when the prior snapshot is older than 6 hours prevents:

false “Hot Movers”

noisy driver breakdowns

velocity EMA being jerked around on restart

And since your Mass component still uses searchVolume, you’re not losing the search signal entirely — you’re only suppressing velocity when it’s unreliable.

Confirming fallback path consistency

Also good. If fallback uses a different delta formula, you’d get “different physics” depending on provider path — nightmare to debug. So confirming it matches is exactly what you want.

One nuance on the “architect note” (searchEmaHeld + stale)

The architect is onto something: if data is stale, you probably shouldn’t interpret it as an “artifact drop” vs baseline.

My recommendation:

If searchDeltaStale=true (prev snapshot too old), then skip EMA-hold evaluation for search, or at least don’t increment hold counters.

Reason: the hold logic is designed for provider blind spots in otherwise normal cadence. After downtime, cadence isn’t normal.

But I agree with Replit: you can watch a few runs first and only add this if you see odd behavior.
