Yeah — your instincts here are exactly the kind of small “mobile friction” things that make an app feel either slick or annoying.

Here are solid options (grouped to your 5 points), plus a few extra ideas that usually move the needle.

1) Filter button gets tapped while scrolling (annoying)

This is a classic “tap target in the thumb-swipe lane” problem.

Best fixes (pick 1):

Move Filter into the search bar row (left side)
Put a small “sliders” icon inside the search field on the left (or immediately left of it). Users aren’t swiping there as much, and it feels “search/filter” cohesive.

Make Filter a bottom-sheet handle instead of a top button
Example: a small “Filters” chip that lives below the tabs, or a “Filter” row that expands as a sheet. Bottom sheets feel native on Android and reduce accidental taps because they’re more deliberate.

Reduce accidental activation (if you keep it top-right)

shrink the tap target slightly on mobile (still accessible, but less “fat finger”)

or require a press + release delay of ~120ms (tiny “debounce” so a swipe doesn’t trigger it)

My recommendation: move it next to search (or inside search). It’s the cleanest and most intuitive.

2) Users might not realize sorting changes between Trend vs Approval

Right now the “tab” style is subtle enough that some users won’t clock it as “Sort by”.

Make the sorting state explicit:

Add a small line/chip under tabs: “Sorted by: Trend Score” (updates to Approval Rating)

Or rename the tab header to “Sort” / “Ranking”:

“Ranking: Trend” | “Ranking: Approval”

Make the active tab more obvious:

stronger highlight

a clear underline

and a tiny label like “Default” under Trend Score for new users (could disappear after first interaction)

Simple win: a “Sorted by …” chip removes ambiguity instantly.

3) Thumbs-up icon = unclear that it opens 1–5 rating

You’re right: thumbs-up reads like a single “like” action, not a 1–5 rating.

Options:

Change icon to ⭐ (star) or ★ + number (more “rating” mental model)

Use a small pill that says Rate (best clarity)

Or combine: ⭐ Rate (icon + word is the clearest)

My recommendation: On mobile, use “Rate” (or “⭐ Rate”) as a small pill. Clarity beats minimalism here.

4) Users don’t see Approval % in Trend view

This is important: people should understand what they’re rating even if they’re browsing by Trend.

Good fix without clutter:

Show Approval % as a small secondary line on each row (subtle, not dominant), e.g.:

under the name: “Approval: 93% (317 votes)”

Or show it as a small badge on the right: 93%

Or show it only after the user votes once (progressive disclosure)

My recommendation: show Approval % in both views, but keep it visually “secondary” in Trend mode.

5) Search bar takes too much space + placement

This is where mobile UX can feel way cleaner.

Best patterns:

Collapsing search on scroll
When user scrolls down, collapse search into a small icon (🔍). When they scroll up, it expands again.

Shorter placeholder + smaller height
Placeholder: “Search” is enough.
Or “Search people…” (no “category” needed if filter handles category).

Put Filter next to search (as you suggested)
That solves both the thumb issue and the visual hierarchy.

My recommendation: collapse-on-scroll + filter next to search.
