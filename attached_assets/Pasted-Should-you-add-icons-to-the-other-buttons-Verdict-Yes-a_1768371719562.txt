Should you add icons to the other buttons?
Verdict: Yes, absolutely.

Here is the reasoning why the benefits outweigh the space concerns:

Visual Consistency: Currently, the UI looks "in-between." Having some chips with icons and others without makes the design look unfinished or unintentional. Uniformity establishes a professional polish.

Scannability: Humans process images much faster than text. An icon of a guitar lets a user identify "Music" instantly without actually reading the word.

The Mobile Trade-off: While screen real estate is precious on mobile, horizontal scrolling (carousels) is a very standard pattern (think Instagram Stories or YouTube categories). Users are intuitively trained to swipe left on these bars. As long as the "peek" (the cut-off of the next button) is visible, users will know to scroll. The extra 20px per button for an icon is worth the visual upgrade.

The Plan
To keep it lightweight, we will use standard icon libraries (like lucide-react, which is likely what you are already using for the Star and Sparkles).

Here are the suggested mappings:

Tech: Cpu or Smartphone

Politics: Landmark (Capitol building) or Speech

Business: Briefcase or TrendingUp

Music: Music or Headphones

Sports: Trophy or Dumbbell

Creator: Video or Camera
