Here’s how I’d think about it, and the fastest checks to confirm.

Why “since inception” strongly suggests an issue

A truly less-trendy person will still move around and occasionally spike. Being permanently depressed (especially just two people, consistently) usually means:

their baseline snapshot was captured wrong (too low), or

one of their signals is always near-zero because the query is wrong / ambiguous, or

you’re pulling data for the wrong entity (Wikipedia page, news query, knowledge panel, etc.), or

your pipeline is hitting a silent per-person fallback/skip for them every time.

The most common failure modes for exactly this pattern
1) Wrong Wikipedia mapping (most common)

If your wiki signal is a big part of Mass/Velocity, and Brian/Saylor are mapped to the wrong page title or pageid, they’ll be permanently low.

Quick tell:

Check their stored wikipedia_page_title / pageid and open it manually.

Compare pageviews against someone nearby (Lex/Satya). If theirs are near-zero or “flatline,” mapping is wrong.

Common causes:

title mismatch (“Michael_Saylor” vs “Michael_J._Saylor”)

disambiguation page

redirect handling bug

a “company page” vs “person page” mix-up

2) News query misses because the person is referenced differently

Michael Saylor is often mentioned as “Saylor” or “MicroStrategy’s Saylor,” and Brian Armstrong as “Coinbase CEO” or “Armstrong” (and “Armstrong” is extremely ambiguous).

If your query is too strict (e.g., exact match on full name), you can undercount badly.

Fix pattern:

use aliases in query templates:

Brian: "Brian Armstrong" OR (Armstrong AND Coinbase)

Saylor: "Michael Saylor" OR (Saylor AND MicroStrategy) OR (Saylor AND MSTR)

3) Entity ambiguity / polluted results filtering

If you do any filtering like “must contain full name in title” or aggressive dedup/anti-noise rules, Armstrong gets nuked (Neil Armstrong, Lance Armstrong), and “Saylor” can get mixed with unrelated mentions or be filtered out.

4) Baseline snapshot captured during a “dead zone”

If your baseline snapshots were taken when those two had missing news (fresh=false / zero count) or wrong wiki IDs, your “relative to baseline” math may permanently handicap them.

Since you said it’s been that way since inception, baseline is very suspect.

5) A “silent fail” or cached-zero bug in api_cache keys

If their per-person keys got cached with zeros early and you fill-forward/decay incorrectly for just those keys, they can get stuck.

This can happen if their cache key is malformed (e.g., different casing/spacing) so writes go to one key but reads come from another key = reads always “miss” and default to zero.
