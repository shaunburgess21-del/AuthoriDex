First: the math (important)

Your estimate 100 × 24 × 30 = 72,000 calls/month is correct if it’s 1 API call per person per hour.

So with Mediastack 50k/month, you need one of these:

Reduce frequency (e.g., news refresh every 2 hours → ~36k/month)

Reduce coverage per run (e.g., refresh news hourly only for Top 50, rest less often)

Use caching / cooldowns so many people don’t get called every hour

Or upgrade plan later

What I’d integrate (clean + robust)

Treat Mediastack as your primary News provider, but keep the chain you already built:

Mediastack (primary) → GDELT (secondary/free) → Serper News (emergency fallback)

That gives you:

Paid stability most of the time

Free backup if Mediastack hiccups

Serper as “break glass” so you never go dark

The key design decision: Don’t call Mediastack for everyone every hour

Instead, tell Replit to implement tiered fetching + caching:

Recommended fetch policy (fits 50k/month)

Hourly

Top 25–40 people by rank (the people users actually see)

Plus anyone with “news anomaly” (e.g., news suddenly 0 / near-zero while wiki/search strong)

Every 2–4 hours

Everyone else (round-robin so everyone gets refreshed over time)

Caching

If a person’s news was fetched within the last 90–120 minutes, reuse it (no new call)

This will keep you comfortably under 50k while maintaining “feels live” where it matters.

How to compute the signals with Mediastack

Mediastack gives you:

/v1/news endpoint with keywords, date, languages, countries, limit, etc.

It returns a pagination.total you can use as a count estimate for the query window, without paging everything.

Practical approach for your engine

Your engine currently needs something like:

articleCount24h

articleCount7d (or a rolling average / delta)

But Mediastack date is day-based (YYYY-MM-DD or date range).
So the simplest reliable method is:

Query last 2 days (date=yesterday,today) and treat that as your “~24h-ish” pool (or count only items with published_at >= now-24h if you want closer precision, but capped at 100 returned items).

For 7d: don’t make a second request. Instead, compute “7d average” from your own snapshots (rolling mean of the past 7 days of news_count for each person). This saves half your calls immediately.

So: one Mediastack call per person per refresh, not two.

Quick math so you can decide confidently

Assume 1 Mediastack call per person per refresh (recommended; don’t do “24h + 7d” as separate calls).

100 people

Every 2 hours: 100 × 12 × 30 = 36,000/month ✅ fits 50k comfortably (even with retries + a few extras)

Every 1 hour: 100 × 24 × 30 = 72,000/month ❌ exceeds 50k, but ✅ fits 250k easily

Every 30 min: 144,000/month ✅ fits 250k

So:

If you’re happy with 2-hour news freshness, 50k is perfect and still leaves headroom for occasional “per-person retry/fallback” calls.

If you want hourly (or plan to expand beyond 100 people soon), 250k is the “set-and-forget” tier.

My recommendation

Phase 1 (simple + solid): stay on 50k, refresh every 2 hours.
Do it now, stabilize the engine, confirm rankings behave well, and measure how often you actually need extra calls.

Upgrade to 250k when either becomes true:

you want “hourly for everyone” as a product promise, or

you increase tracked people (e.g., 200+), or

you start running “extra” news queries (multiple languages/regions, aliases per person, topic expansion).
