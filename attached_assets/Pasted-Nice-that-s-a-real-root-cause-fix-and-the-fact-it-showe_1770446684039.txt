A few thoughts + what I’d do next to make sure this doesn’t bite you again:

What Replit fixed (and why it matters)

The core bug was “UPDATE fetchedAt but not expiresAt” → cache entries look recently fetched but are already expired (or effectively immortal/invalid depending on how you read them).

Refactoring to a single upsert that always sets fetchedAt + expiresAt together is the right permanent pattern.

Adding both:

write-time guard (never write bad TTLs)

read-time guard (treat legacy bad rows as miss + log)
gives you belt + suspenders.

That should materially improve:

Source health accuracy

Coverage gate correctness

Staleness decay behavior

Your “health summary” trustworthiness
