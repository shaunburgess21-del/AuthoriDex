Yeah — this is a big step forward. The screen reads much clearer now.

1) “Sorted by Trend Score” + arrows: keep the text, simplify the arrows

The “Sorted by: Trend Score” line is doing real work. It tells users what those tabs do without them having to click around.

But I agree: two arrow indicators feels like overkill.

What I’d do:

Keep the arrow only on the active tab (Trend Score or Approval Rating).

On the “Sorted by…” line, remove the arrow and just keep plain text:

Sorted by: Trend Score

(optional) make “Trend Score” a subtle link style that also switches tabs

That gives you one clear “source of truth” visually (the active tab), and one plain-language confirmation (the line).

2) Approval % in rows: it is visually messy + it’s ambiguous

You’re right on both counts:

It adds clutter.

Users will confuse it with the 24h movement % unless it’s explicitly labeled.

Right now you’ve got “score (k) + 24h % + another %” and the second % has no obvious meaning unless you already know.

Best option (cleanest UX)

Only show Approval % when the leaderboard is sorted by Approval Rating.

That keeps the Trend Score view focused and fast-scannable.

If you really want it visible in Trend Score view

Then it needs to be unmistakable:

Use a tiny labeled badge, not a raw percent:

👍 93 (or AR 93)

Or put it on a second line in smaller text:

364k +0.9%

Approval 93%

But personally, I’d avoid it until users are already in “Approval mode”.

My recommendation

✅ Keep Rate button (it’s clearer than 👍)

✅ Keep Sorted by: Trend Score line

✅ Keep filter next to search

🔧 Remove the second arrow (leave arrow only on active tab)

🔧 Remove Approval % from Trend Score rows (show it only in Approval view)

If you want a really nice compromise: in Trend Score view, show nothing about approval except maybe a tiny neutral hint at the top like:

“Want community ratings? Switch to Approval Rating.”

That teaches without cluttering every row.
