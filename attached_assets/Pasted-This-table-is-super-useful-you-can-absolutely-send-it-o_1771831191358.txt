That suggests your “meaningful” definition is currently dominated by rank move ≥ 3 (or it’s being computed against a rank that is more jittery than the score thresholds imply). In other words: your “meaningful” metric is likely over-counting.

What to do: keep “movers ≥2%” as the headline stability signal, and treat “meaningful” as “rank churn” (rename it), or tighten it.

A simple fix:

“Meaningful” = (|pct_change| ≥ 0.5%) AND (|rank_move| ≥ 3)
instead of OR.

Or make two metrics:

meaningfulByScore (score-based)

meaningfulByRank (rank-based)
